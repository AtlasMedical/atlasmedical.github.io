Atlas P1

Intelligent pressure redistribution through variable stiffness technology


Medical Background

Pressure injuries develop due to consistent pressure on a region of tissue and range from a minor irritation to a deep wound.

Specific areas of the body are at higher risk than others.

Problem Background

$11+ billion annually

Pressure injuries increase the cost of treatment due to the need for more intensive monitoring and increased nurse workload.

60,000 Deaths

Patients with a pressure injury are 2.8 times more likely to die in the hospital.

6 Days Longer Hospital Stay

Recovery times increase, leading to patients staying longer and using more hospital resources.

Current Solutions

A subjective risk assessment is completed using a manual rating known as the Braden Scale.

Nurses and caregivers visually inspect the patient's skin to look for signs of pressure injuries, often needing to turn the patient to do so.

If a patient develops a pressure injury, or is at a particularly high risk of doing so, they are manually repositioned as frequently as every hour. This repositioning can take as long as 15 minutes and may require two nurses. Despite all of this labour, this method is imperfect and pressure injuries can still be developed.

Solution Overview

Atlas Medical is developing a variable stiffness mattress to prevent pressure injuries. The stiffness of individual air cells within the mattress are modified using a custom control system.

Data Flow

The pressure sensor and pneumatics make up a true mechatronics system - the design combines software, electrical, and mechanical aspects. Atlas P1 consists of three distinct layers that work together to create a closed loop control system.

Key Innovations

Column Control

A custom printed circuit board serves as the microcontroller interface between the data analysis computer and the sensor. This circuit enables control and measurement of sensor outputs.

Row Control

Another custom printed circuit board consists of a demultiplexer circuit that enables individual control of the sensor rows. The modular design of this circuit board increases robustness and lowers costs.

Statistical Analysis

The sensor information is treated as an image and computer vision techniques are applied to enhance and analyze the signal. The sensor data is upsampled using bicubic interpolation and a characterization process is used to develop a deblurring method that works to partially reverse the load diffusion properties of the air cells.
